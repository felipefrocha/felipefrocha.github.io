I was listening to the latest episode of Next in Tech with Eric and Alex Johnston, discussing their annual survey on AI use cases. The data is fascinating and it highlights a massive wave of investment and strategic intent.

Although "plans" most of the time seams to be solid, there are few things that I couldn't help but think about the silent killers of execution that a high-level survey often misses.

We see organizations eager to adopt, yet many walk into a trap because they confuse Availability with Readiness.

Here is where the gap between "Strategy" and "Engineering Reality" usually breaks the project:

1. The TRL (Technology Readiness Level) Blind Spot Adopting AI isn’t just about buying a license; 

Map the TRL of the solution to your specific workflow. Don't ignore readiness, you don't just get "friction", by the path of least resistance you end up with Shadow AI or "smart" adjustments. 

Teams will try their best to deliver the requirements imposed over imature tools, or bypass governance entirely to get things done, creating a debt spiral that is hard to reverse.

2. The "Huge Model" Bias There is a pervasive bias that a "Huge Model" (LLM) is the hammer for every nail. This leads to an obsession with model size while neglecting the basics: Data Quality.

- If you don't understand the nature of your problem, an LLM is just a very expensive way to generate noise.

- Often, a smaller, clearer model with pristine data outperforms a massive model on messy data.

3. Deep Workflow Implants vs. Surface Features AI isn't a plugin; it's a deep implant into daily workflows. An inconsistent strategy that treats AI as a "feature" rather than a fundamental shift in how data moves will inevitably fail.

**The Takeaway:** 

Strategy looks great on a survey. But success lives in the boring basics: assessing TRLs honestly, curbing Shadow IT through better enablement, prioritizing data quality, not abuse of the latest big model with messy use cases.
